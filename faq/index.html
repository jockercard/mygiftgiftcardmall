Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about your gift card

← Back to Home

How do I activate my card?

Visit the home page and use the Register Card tab. Enter your 16-digit card number, expiration date, and security code (CVV) when prompted. Once registered, you can sign in to check your balance and manage your card.

How can I check my balance?

Go to the Check Balance tab on the home page. Enter your card number, expiration date (MM/YY), and CVV (3-digit security code on the back). Your available balance will be displayed after verification. Balance checks are free and unlimited.

Where can I use my gift card?

Your card can be used anywhere Debit Mastercard or Visa debit cards are accepted in the United States and District of Columbia — online, in stores, for bill payments, and at ATMs (if your card allows cash access).

Why did my online purchase fail even though I have funds?

Most likely the card didn't have a billing address registered, or the merchant only accepts certain card types. Register an address using the Register Card option, then try again.

What if my balance check fails?

Confirm your card number, expiration date, and CVV are entered correctly. Ensure your card is activated. If the problem continues, contact us for assistance.

Do the funds on my card expire?

Card terms vary. The card's plastic may show an expiration date, but your funds often don't expire in the same way. After the date, you might need a replacement card to access remaining funds. See your Cardholder Agreement for details.

Can I reload the card?

Most Visa and Mastercard gift cards are non-reloadable. Once the balance is used, the card is finished.

How do I personalize my PIN?

Use the Personalize PIN tab on the home page. Enter your card details and follow the prompts. A PIN is useful for "debit" transactions at payment terminals.

What if my card is lost or stolen?

Contact support immediately through the number on the back of your card. You'll usually need the card number or proof of purchase to request a replacement.

Are there monthly fees?

Some cards charge inactivity fees after a certain period. Check the Cardholder Agreement for your specific card for fee details.
