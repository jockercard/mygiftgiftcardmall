How to Activate Your Card

  1. Find the activation sticker on the front of your card or packaging. If it says "Activation required," you'll need to complete this step before making purchases.
  2. Go to the home page and use the Register Card tab. Enter your 16-digit card number, expiration date (MM/YY), and the CVV (3 digits on the back).
  3. Set a 4-digit PIN if offered — this is useful for "debit" transactions at payment terminals.
  4. Register a billing address. This is important for online shopping, where websites verify your payment against the card's billing address.
  5. Wait a moment if needed, then test with a small purchase to confirm everything works.
Tip: Some cards are activated at checkout by the cashier, so you might already be done. If you're not sure, try the online activation to be safe.

How to Check Your Balance

  1. Go to the home page and select the Check Balance tab.
  2. Enter your 16-digit card number, expiration date, and CVV.
  3. View your available balance. Note that gas stations, hotels, and delivery services may place temporary "holds" that reduce your usable amount.
  4. Use the transaction history to monitor recent purchases and check for any unfamiliar charges.
Split payments: If your balance is less than your total, ask the store to split the payment — charge the gift card first, then pay the remainder with another method.

Use Online

Your gift card is a simple, safe, and convenient method of making online payments. You can use it at any website or app that accepts Visa or Mastercard debit cards.

When shopping online, enter your card number, expiration date, and security code (CVV) just as you would with any other debit or credit card. Your card is protected by the same security standards as other payment cards.

Always use secure, trusted websites and make sure you've registered a billing address on your card for smooth checkout.

ATM Access

Your gift card may allow you to withdraw cash at ATMs and get cash back at the point of sale, depending on your card type and the Cardholder Agreement.

Fees may apply for ATM withdrawals or for using out-of-network ATMs. Check your Cardholder Agreement for fee details and daily limits.

Note: Most Visa gift cards are designed for purchases, not cash advances. Don't attempt ATM withdrawals unless your card terms explicitly allow it.

Use Everywhere*

Your gift card can be used everywhere Debit Mastercard and Visa debit cards are accepted within the United States and the District of Columbia. That includes retail stores, gas stations, online shopping, bill payments, and more.

Some card programs may have restrictions (for example, online-only or certain merchant categories). See your Cardholder Agreement for full details on where your card can be used.

*Terms and eligibility depend on your specific card program. Funds and usage are subject to the Cardholder Agreement.
